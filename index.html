Abstract

We propose a novel controllable text-to-speech (TTS) method that controls voice characteristics via text descriptions, eliminating the need for audio-description pairs in the target language. Our method uses an unlabeled audio-only dataset to obtain disentangled timbre representations through self-supervised learning (SSL). We then use a dataset of the target language to train a TTS model, utilizing an SSL-based style encoder to extract style embeddings. Lastly, we train a model to map text descriptions to the timbre and style embeddings using an audio-description paired dataset of another language. Since SSL-based timbre and style representation extractors can be considered language-agnostic, they enable the transfer of description-based control across different languages. Experiments on English and Japanese TTS show that our method enables high naturalness and controllability for both languages, even without audio-description pairs for Japanese data.
